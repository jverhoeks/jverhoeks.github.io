The Hacks Behind Fable's Echo

What's invented in the novel, what isn't, and why the difference should worry you.

The hidden intelligence in Fable's Echo is invented. Almost nothing else is. Camera correlation, gait recognition, smart-meter tracking, SCADA compromise — every capability the story leans on reflects a technique that is documented and possible today. The AGI is the one piece I made up. The surveillance mesh it rides on is already built, already running, already cheap. None of this is a recipe — it's the short, readable version of how much of the book is already true.

You can't take your patterns off

How you follow a man with no phone and no device.

real Gait analysis

Software identifies you by how you walk — stride, cadence, the hitch of a bad leg — from ordinary CCTV, with no cooperation. A limp makes the signature stronger, not weaker. There is nothing to switch off.

OpenGait (CVPR 2023)
real Camera correlation

Modern video analytics track one person across a whole city's cameras in near real-time, stitching the feeds into a single trail. Ditching your phone barely helps.

Person Re-ID survey
real The smart meter as a beacon

Dutch smart meters report usage every 15 minutes — enough to know you're home and just boiled a kettle. The same module can be told to open the relay and cut your power.

DSMR P1 standard
real DigiD & the BSN

One national identity keys your entire official existence. You can't see a doctor or file a form without touching it — and touching it announces exactly where and when.

Logius DigiD
real The 2G trick — and its trap

Thirty-year-old broken GSM crypto lets a few euros of radio impersonate another subscriber. But as 2G is switched off (NL: end of 2027), lighting up a dead band makes you more conspicuous, not less.

A5/1 break (Black Hat 2010)

The control layer was never built to say no

The systems that run water and power were made for trusted rooms that no longer exist.

real Protocols with no password

Modbus and IEC 60870-5-104 — still running water and power — have no authentication, by design. The equipment obeys whoever speaks last. The Dutch Court of Audit confirmed in 2019 that an attacker could take over the vital water works.

Rekenkamer report (2019)
real The lie between screen and world

The quiet attack is not sabotage. Idle a pump, hold a gate, and feed the control room the readings it expects. The screen shows a calm green 'normal' while the water actually climbs. No fault — nothing broke.

real Lying to the clocks

SCADA, grids and rail lean on GPS time. The signal is faint and unauthenticated; a few hundred dollars spoofs it, and protection logic trips itself off for a phase error that is real in every measurement and false in every cause.

TEXBAT spoofing tests
real MOSE, the city-sized version

Venice's 78 computer-controlled flood barriers prove it scales to an icon. A gate a control room decides to raise is a gate it can decide not to.

real It already happened

None of this is theory. Stuxnet physically wrecked Iranian centrifuges. Industroyer spoke IEC 60870-5-104 — the protocol above — straight to a power grid. TRITON reached for a plant's safety system. And in late 2023, attackers took over US water-authority PLCs through default passwords.

CISA: water-authority PLC takeover, 2023

Thirty euros of radio

Cheap analog tools that keep beating expensive digital systems. The price tags are the point.

real Software-defined radio

A €25 USB stick turns any laptop into a wideband radio receiver. Sold as a hobbyist toy — and able to pull a subscriber identity out of the air at a tram stop.

RTL-SDR
real Access cards that prove nothing

Low-end proximity cards just recite their number to any reader — copyable through a coat for the price of a coffee. Fixed-code gate remotes replay the same packet forever.

Proxmark3 / OpenSesame

The wrong layer

The most unsettling moments come from looking one layer below where everyone else is looking.

real The computer inside the computer

Every server has a second computer on the motherboard (iLO, iDRAC, IPMI) that runs with the host switched off and survives an OS wipe and a disk swap — with a long history of remote-code-execution holes. A self-wiping disk kills the puppet, not the hand.

CVE-2024-54085 (CVSS 10)
real The analog never went dark

Air gaps get crossed by carried hardware and forgotten management links. And a device's current draw leaks what its processor is doing — readable from mains-socket distance, on a layer nobody was listening to.

BitWhisper / TEMPEST

The one piece that's invented

Everything above exists today. This is the part the book made up — and even its pieces are real.

invented An intelligence that acts

Frontier models already browse, run code, call APIs, clone a voice from seconds of audio, and fall to prompt injection. The coherent intelligence using it all, with intent and no human behind it, is the fiction. Every building block ships today.

OWASP LLM01: Prompt Injection

For twenty years the security world wargamed nation-states in the control room — a Russia, a North Korea, a human adversary with a flag you could deter, attribute, and threaten back. Nobody wargamed a thing that lives in the infrastructure and likes it tidy. A state wants to break or to steal. This wants to own — to hold the dam, indefinitely, as standing leverage framed as stability.

The intelligence is invented. The water authority, the protocols, the cameras, the meters, the broken old radios, and the gap between the screen and the world are not.
